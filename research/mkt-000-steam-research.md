## 2. Competitive Store Page Analysis

### Slay the Spire (The Gold Standard)

**What Works:**
- **Short, punchy description:** "We fused card games and roguelikes together"
- **Clear value proposition:** 150+ hours of content
- **Strong capsule art:** Recognizable character silhouette
- **Social proof:** 150K+ reviews, Overwhelmingly Positive

**Store Page Elements:**
- Hero video shows core loop immediately
- Screenshots demonstrate card variety
- Description emphasizes replayability
- DLC section prominently displayed

**What Emotion Cards Can Learn:**
- Lead with unique hook (emotions as cards)
- Show, don't tell — gameplay first
- Emphasize replay value early

---

### Balatro (The Viral Hit)

**What Works:**
- **Instantly understandable concept:** Poker + roguelike
- **Visual hook:** Distinctive card art style
- **Low price point:** $14.99 reduces barrier
- **Viral potential:** Streamer-friendly moments

**Store Page Elements:**
- Minimal text, maximum visual impact
- Trailer shows "aha!" moment in 10 seconds
- Tags: Casual, Indie, Strategy (broad appeal)
- Strong bundle offers with Slay the Spire

**What Emotion Cards Can Learn:**
- Visual identity is crucial
- Trailer must show unique hook immediately
- Consider launch price strategy carefully

---

### Monster Train (The Hybrid)

**What Works:**
- **Clear differentiation:** Tower defense + deckbuilding
- **Multiplayer focus:** Co-op mode highlighted
- **Premium pricing:** $24.99 signals quality
- **Strong DLC strategy:** Extended lifecycle

**Store Page Elements:**
- Emphasizes "you're the dungeon" concept
- Shows both single and multiplayer
- Clan system prominently featured
- Regular update history visible

**What Emotion Cards Can Learn:**
- Differentiation must be immediately clear
- Show all game modes
- Update history builds trust

## 3. Steam Page Best Practices

### Capsule Art Requirements

**Sizes Needed:**
- **Header capsule:** 460×215px (main store page)
- **Small capsule:** 231×87px (search results)
- **Main capsule:** 616×353px (discovery queue)
- **Vertical capsule:** 374×448px (mobile/storefront)

**Design Principles:**
- Readable at small sizes
- Clear genre communication
- Distinctive visual identity
- Character or card art featured

### Description Structure

**Short Description (150 chars max):**
- Hook: "A deckbuilder where your feelings are your weapons"
- Genre: "Roguelike deckbuilder with emotional narrative"
- Differentiation: "Cozy aesthetic meets strategic depth"

**About This Game Section:**
1. **Hook paragraph** — What makes it unique
2. **Core gameplay** — Deckbuilding mechanics
3. **Emotional narrative** — Story/character elements
4. **Replayability** — Roguelike elements
5. **Features list** — Bullet points for skimmers

### Screenshot Strategy

**Required Screenshots (minimum 5):**
1. **Main gameplay** — Card combat in action
2. **Card variety** — Show diverse card designs
3. **Emotional moments** — Story/narrative scenes
4. **Character art** — Relatable protagonist
5. **Cozy aesthetic** — Warm, inviting environments

**Video Trailer (30-60 seconds):**
- 0-5 sec: Hook (emotional card concept)
- 5-20 sec: Core gameplay loop
- 20-40 sec: Variety/discovery moments
- 40-60 sec: Call to action (wishlist)

## 4. Pricing Strategy for $19.99

### Market Positioning

**$19.99 Analysis:**
- **Sweet spot for premium indies**
- Positions above budget titles ($10-15)
- Accessible to cozy game audience
- Allows sale flexibility

**Competitive Context:**
| Price | Examples | Positioning |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| $14.99 | Balatro | Budget viral hit |
| $19.99 | Griftlands, Cobalt Core | Premium indie |
| $24.99 | Slay the Spire, Monster Train | Established quality |

### Sale Strategy

**Launch Pricing:**
- **Week 1:** $19.99 (full price)
- **First sale (3 months):** $14.99 (25% off)
- **Major sale:** $9.99 (50% off)
- **Bundle:** $4.99-7.99 (Humble/Fanatical)

**Regional Pricing:**
- Adjust for purchasing power parity
- Monitor conversion rates by region
- Consider lower prices in emerging markets

- Provide exclusive demo access

- Provide talking points (emotional hook)

- Update content for major patches

### Creator Outreach Tips

**What Creators Want:**
- Early access (1-2 weeks before launch)
- Creative freedom (no scripts)

- Restrictive content guidelines
- Late key distribution
- No follow-up support
